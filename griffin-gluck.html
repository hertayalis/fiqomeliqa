Griffin Griffin Gluck, born Alexander Gluck, is an American vicenarian actor. He is well known for his roles as Charlie in the “Red Band Society” series, Danny Gannon in “Back in the Game,” and many others.

Where was Griffin Gluck born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Griffin Gluck was born on August 24, 2000, in Los Angeles, California, to film director and producer Cellini Gluck and production assistant and line producer Karin Beck. His eldest sister, Caroline Paris Gluck, is also an actress.

He is said to have gone to Oak Park High School in Oak Park, California. He is of Japanese and Austrian Jewish ancestry on his father’s side, and English, German, Scottish, Polish, and Irish ancestry on his mother’s side.

How did Griffin Gluck start his Professional Career?

Gluck made his television debut as a Pharmacy kid in the 2009 film “Sideways aka Saidoweizu.” In 2010, he played Half Bred in “The Office” episode “WUPHF.com.”

He also appeared in “The United States of Tara,” where he played Monty in three episodes, and “Private Practice,” where he played Mason Warner from seasons 5 to 6.

In 2013, he played Philip in the film “Trust Me,” and Danny Gannon in the television series “Back in the Game.” In the 2014 film “Just Before I Go,” he portrayed Randy Morgan.

He also appeared in an episode of the TV show “Silicon Valley” as an Adderall boy. He starred as Charlie in the teen medical comedy-drama series “Red Band Society” the same year. In the 2015 film “Batman vs. Robin,” he played Young Bruce Wayne.

He also appeared in three series: “About a Boy” as Clay in three episodes, “Cuckoo” as Dylan in a TV pilot, and “Life in Pieces” as Aiden in one episode. He played Sam Ecklund in the mockumentary series “American Vandal” in 2017.

In 2019, he starred in the comedy film “Big Time Adolescence” as Monroe “Mo” Harris. In the film “Tall Girl,” he also played Jack Dunkleman. He starred as Gabe in the television series “Locke & Key” in 2020. He also appeared on the TV show “The Real Bros of Simi Valley” as Aaron.
