Who Is Kamalani Dung?

Kamalani Dung is an American professional softball pitcher, who plays for the Puerto Rican National Team. She is also a gold medalist, who has competed in various tournaments.

Likewise, Dung is famously known for being the former rumored girlfriend of Shohei Ohtani, a Japanese professional baseball pitcher.

Kamalani Dung- Bio, Age, Parents, Siblings, Ethnicity, Education

Softball pitcher Kamalani was born in Waianae, Hawaii, United States on 4 March 1997. As of 2023, her age is 26 and holds an American nationality.

Similarly, she is of Caucasian ethnicity and according to the astrological chart, her birth sign is Pisces.

Following her parents, Dung was born to father, Lance Dung, and mother, Honey Rodrigues. She has one brother named, Lancen Dung.

As per her academics,  she completed her schooling at the Kamehameha Schools. Later, she graduated from the University of California.

Kamalani Dung- Professional Life, Careers

During her junior career, she made her debut for the Cal in the Pac-12 Conference. Dung was able to book her place as an ace pitcher with the Bears.

Altogether, she played 33 games scoring PAC 12: ERA (1.96), wins (19), batters struck out (186), innings pitched (160.2), opposing batting average (.181)

She got in the nomination for the Malihini Kipa Aloha All-Tournament Team as a most valuable pitcher.

As a senior was named to the NFCA All-Region Third Team and received All-Conference honors.

She contributed for the Golden Bears to their first top 25 wins. She batted 12 strikes and two hits during a victory game against Duke University, which got her fame as a top 9 performer that week.

International Career

Being a half-Puerto Rican, she got an honorable opportunity to play for the 2018 Puerto Rico Women’s National Softball Team. Dung was named to the 2018 U.S. International Cup and the 2018 Women’s Softball World Championship Tournament.

Kamalani was named in the 2019 World Cup of Softball, 2019 Canada Cup, 2019 Pan American Games,  and 2019 Tokyo 2020 Americas Olympic Qualifier.

While playing for Puerto Rico, she became the winner of the Bronze medal at the 2019 Pan American Games held in Lima, Peru.

Currently, the talented softball player plays for the Athletes Unlimited Professional Sports League in the United States.
