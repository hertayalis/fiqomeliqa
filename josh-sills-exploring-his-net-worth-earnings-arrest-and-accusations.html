Josh Sills: Exploring His Net Worth, Earnings, Arrest, and Accusations

Josh Sills’ net worth, career earnings, and achievements have piqued the curiosity of many. If you’re among those interested, we’ve got you covered. Josh Sills, an offensive lineman for the Philadelphia Eagles in the National Football League, rose to prominence as an undrafted free agent and has been playing for the club. However, he gained attention after facing accusations of abduction and rape. With less than two weeks until the Super Bowl, the club is gearing up for their match.

The Philadelphia Eagles will be facing off against the Kansas City Chiefs in the Super Bowl on February 12 in Arizona. Read on to discover more about Josh Sills’ career, accomplishments, and net worth.

Earnings from His Career and Net Worth Before Arrest

As per Spotrac, Josh Sills signed a three-year, $2,570,00 deal with the Philadelphia Eagles in January 2022, which included a $10,000 signing bonus. His annual average pay stands at $856,667. Sills, who has only been playing professionally for a year, managed to secure a spot in the Eagles’ final roster alongside Reed Blankenship and Josh Jobe as undrafted players. In his rookie year, Sills appeared in a single game and made several special teams plays against the Arizona Cardinals in week five.

During his debut season, the player earned $715,000, indicating that his net worth is likely substantial. However, following his arrest, the NFL placed him on the commissioner’s exempt list, barring him from participating in Eagles’ practices or games.

Early Career and Achievements

Born on January 26, 1998, Josh Sills, a 25-year-old from Sarahsville, Ohio, attended Meadowbrook High School in Byesville, where he was a two-year starter. After committing to West Virginia University, he redshirted his first year. In his rookie year in 2017, he appeared in 13 games, with 10 starts split between left and right guard. He led his squad with thirteen knockdown blocks and took 681 snaps. Sills continued to excel in 2018, starting 12 games and making 970 plays, earning himself second-team All-Big 12 Conference honors.

In 2019, he appeared in two games and made two starts before being sidelined for the rest of the season. Sills then transferred to Oklahoma State University for the 2020 season, where he started all 11 games, securing a spot on the first-team All-Big 12 Conference. In January 2021, Sills announced his return for his senior season, during which he was voted team captain and earned a position on the Big 12 first team for the second year in a row. He was also nominated for the Big 12 Offensive Lineman of the Year award.

Accusations and Arrest

Josh Sills, the offensive lineman for the Philadelphia Eagles, faced accusations of detaining a victim and engaging in nonconsensual sexual behavior. The alleged incident, as per a statement by Ohio Attorney General Dave Yost and Guernsey County Sheriff Jeffrey Paden, took place on December 5, 2019. The footballer was summoned to court on February 16.

Following the news breaking, both the NFL and the Eagles released statements. The Eagles acknowledged the legal situation and mentioned discussions with the league administration. Similarly, the NFL stated that they would assess the matter under their conduct rules and informed the public about Sills being placed on the Commissioner Exempt list.
